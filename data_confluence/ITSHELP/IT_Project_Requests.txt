ITS has an intake process to review IT requests and develop proposals for approved projects.
Process
Initial request
Complete a brief
ITS Project Intake form
(requires login to ITS Service Portal)
Initial ITS Management Review
ITS management will review the request and assign a liaison.  If the project receives initial approval, the liaison will work with the requestor to develop a project proposal document.
If the project does not receive initial approval, the ITS liaison will discuss the reasons with the requestor.
Project Proposal
The ITS liaison and requestor will work collaboratively to develop a project proposal that will include items such as:
Project scope
Security and accessibility reviews.  In many cases this will involve requesting
Higher Education Community Vendor Assessment
(HECVAT) and
Voluntary Product Accessibility Template
(VPAT) documents from vendors.  These are standard documents that aid in assessing vendor compliance with security and accessibility best practices.
Data management
plan
Integration requirements
Timeline
Budget requirements and ongoing costs
Staffing requirements
Final Approval
When complete, the ITS liaison will submit the project proposal to ITS management for review.  The outcome of the review can be:
Accept the project, set prioritization, and assign resources.
Reject the project.  The ITS liaison will communicate the decision to the requestor and discuss why the project was not approved.
Defer project to a later time or ask for more research or information.
