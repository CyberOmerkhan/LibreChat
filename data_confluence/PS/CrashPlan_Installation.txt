Swarthmore College's Backup Solution - CrashPlan
Note:
This software is not optimized for labs or some research machines. Don't hesitate to contact the
Academic Technology
team for a potential alternate backup solution.
Step 1: CrashPlan Installation
You may already have CrashPlan installed.  If so, you can skip to
Step 2
.
Download the appropriate version of the CrashPlan software below.  The installation process may take up to 10 minutes.
CrashPlan for Windows
CrashPlan for MacOS
CrashPlan for Linux
(
Linux installation Instructions
)
To sign in to CrashPlan:
the computer's primary user will need to log in with their Swarthmore network credentials (
with
the @
swarthmore.edu
at the end).
The CrashPlan server address is:
clients.us2.crashplan.com
After installation, CrashPlan may require an update for you to sign in again.
Step 2: Launch and Sign into CrashPlan
Once CrashPlan is installed, open the CrashPlan application.
How to locate:
Windows
:  Click the start button and type “Crash” in the search. Then click on the CrashPlan icon.
macOS
: Click on Applications and locate CrashPlan.  Or search for "CrashPlan" in Spotlight (magnifying glass icon) in the upper right corner.
To sign in to CrashPlan
:
the computer's primary user will need to log in with their Swarthmore network credentials (
with
the @swarthmore.edu at the end).
The CrashPlan server address is:
clients.us2.crashplan.com
After the initial sign-in, the setup will prompt your web browser to launch and require your Swarthmore sign in, (
without
the @
swarthmore.edu
at the end)
Once logged in, click on
Start Backup.
Step 3: Confirmation
Please get in touch with Support Services after installation so we can verify your backup (information is located at the bottom).  Please save any critical files onto your Swarthmore Google Drive and/or an external drive until we confirm that your CrashPlan backup is running smoothly.
