Info
This article only applies to you if you own a top-level domain. If you have been using the free subdomain option with
domains.swarthmore.edu
through Swarthmore College, this does
not
apply.
Similarly, if you’re
migrating your content to Reclaim Hosting
, this article does
not
apply to you.
Transferring a domain you already own is not too different from registering a new domain, but the transfer process requires an
EPP code
, or an agreement code between your old registrar and your new registrar that allows the release of your domain. Your new registrar will have information on how to transfer a domain. When you start that process, you will be prompted to enter your EPP code.
How to find your EPP Code
:
To get started, you’ll need to log in to your control panel (
https://domains.swarthmore.edu/dashboard
) using your Digital Swarthmore username and password.
Once you’re logged in, you’ll see the cPanel interface.  From here, click on the
Manage Your Account
menu at the top of your screen and select
Migration Information.
Click on the
Lock
button to unlock your Swarthmore College account.
Select
Get Code
. Once that’s done, the system will send you an email with your EPP code.
At this stage, here are a few items to note:
You’ll receive a series of emails from both your old and new registrars asking you to authorize the transfer. Please act on
every
email you receive in a timely fashion, even if the emails look like duplicates.
If you do not authorize the transfer in a timely fashion, the domain transfer will expire, and you will need to start over.
The domain must be older than 60 days to be transferable.
The domain must have no other transfers in the last 60 days.
The domain transfer process can take up to a week, depending on how fast your registrars work.
Once the domain transfer completes, you’ll receive a notification.
